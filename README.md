# Inside Modular Interiors Analytics – Part 1

## Lead Funnel & Conversion Analysis

### Project Overview

Built as Part 1 of the Inside Modular Interiors Analytics series, which explores sales, marketing, operations, and profitability across a modular furniture business.
This Power BI project analyzes the lead to customer journey for a modular furniture business. The objective was to understand how leads progress through the sales process, identify high-performing lead sources, evaluate customer segments, and uncover opportunities to improve conversion performance.

## Business Questions

This analysis was conducted to answer the following questions:

* Where is the sales pipeline concentrated?
* Which lead sources perform best?
* Which budget segments convert best?
* Which customer segments convert best?


## Dashboard Pages

### Lead Conversion Analysis

Analyzed pipeline stages to identify lead concentration and conversion bottlenecks.

### Lead Source Performance Analysis

Compared lead volume, conversions, and conversion rates across acquisition channels.

### Budget Segment Analysis

Evaluated conversion performance across different customer budget segments.

### Customer Segment Analysis

Analyzed conversion outcomes across cities and project types.

### Executive Summary & Recommendations

Summarized key findings and recommended business actions.


## Key Insights

* 41.8% of leads remain in the Inquiry stage, making it the largest bottleneck in the sales process.
* Referral and Walk-in channels delivered the strongest conversion performance.
* Mid-budget customers generated the highest lead volume and customer conversions.
* Kitchen Storage recorded the highest conversion rate among all project categories.
* Conversion performance varied across cities and project types, highlighting opportunities for targeted marketing efforts.


## Recommendations

* Improve follow-up processes for Inquiry-stage leads.
* Increase focus on Referral and Walk-in lead generation.
* Continue targeting Mid-budget customers.
* Promote high-performing project categories such as Kitchen Storage.
* Use city and project-type performance insights to guide marketing campaigns.
